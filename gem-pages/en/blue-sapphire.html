Blue Sapphire

The Eternal Symbol of Wisdom and Royalty

Ceylon blue sapphires are prized worldwide for their exceptional cornflower-to-royal blue hues and remarkable durability. Sourced from Sri Lanka's legendary mines, these gemstones are highly sought after in Australia for engagement rings and heirloom pieces.

Colour & Quality Guide

Colour

Most desirable are vivid royal blue to cornflower blue with even saturation. Avoid overly dark or grey-tinged stones.

Clarity

Eye-clean stones are preferred, though minor silk inclusions (rutile needles) are acceptable and natural in Ceylon sapphires.

Treatment

Most sapphires are heat-treated to enhance colour‚Äîthis is standard and accepted. Unheated stones command premium prices.

Certification

Available upon request from GIA, IGI, or CGL laboratories.

Cultural & Traditional Significance

  • Birthstone: September
  • Associated with: Wisdom, loyalty, protection
  • Planetary Link: Saturn (Vedic astrology - brings discipline and focus)

Blue sapphire is revered in Chinese culture as a symbol of heaven and divine favour, particularly popular among Australian-Chinese communities.

Investment & Market Appeal in Australia

Blue sapphires have shown consistent appreciation in the Australian market, particularly fine Ceylon stones. With growing demand for coloured gemstone engagement rings and Princess Catherine's iconic sapphire ring inspiring global trends, investment-grade blue sapphires remain highly collectible. Australian buyers increasingly value ethical sourcing and certification.
